# SiteForge Business System

## What SiteForge actually is

SiteForge is **not just a website template**.

It is a business system for finding, validating, selling, building, and maintaining websites for local service businesses.

The product is best understood as:

> a local business growth system built around a high-converting website, local SEO structure, and ongoing content/maintenance

So the real offer is not:
- "we make websites"

The real offer is:
- better visibility
- more tarjouspyynnöt
- more calls
- stronger local trust
- ongoing improvement without the client needing to manage the site themselves

---

## Who it is for

The best customers are:
- local service businesses
- businesses that sell through trust + visibility + contact requests
- companies where phone calls and tarjouspyynnöt matter
- businesses that are real operators in one city or region
- businesses that are underpowered digitally

### Strong-fit examples
- sähköurakoitsija
- LVI-yritys
- saumausyritys
- putkiliike
- remonttifirma
- kattotyöt
- pienurakoitsijat
- muut paikalliset rakennusalan palveluyritykset

### Weak-fit examples
- ecommerce businesses
- chains
- enterprise operators
- highly complex booking-driven businesses
- businesses that already have a strong digital engine

---

## The core problem SiteForge solves

Many local service businesses are good at the actual work but weak online.

Typical situation:
- business is real
- customers exist
- company may even have reviews and reputation
- but website is weak, outdated, missing, or under-converting
- no strong CTA
- no trust structure
- no local SEO depth
- no content engine
- no ongoing maintenance rhythm

This creates a gap between:
- how good the business actually is
- and how good it looks / converts online

SiteForge exists to close that gap.

## The business model

### Offer
The offer is framed as:

> “Lisää tarjouspyyntöjä, soittoja ja näkyvyyttä.”

Not just:
- “new website”

### Price model
Current working model:
- **Setup:** €1200
- **Monthly:** €99 / month
- **Contract:** 12 months

That means:
- €1200 upfront
- €1188 annual recurring per client

### Revenue logic
Example target:
- 20 active clients × €99 / month = **€1980 MRR**
- plus setup fees from new clients

This is not yet the whole €25k/month mission by itself, but it can become:
- a real recurring base
- a service channel
- a proof of repeatable acquisition + delivery
- a foundation for a more scalable local-business growth product

## The delivery model

### Stage 1 — Discovery
Find businesses we can genuinely help.

This now uses:
- maps-first / places-first discovery
- not search-first discovery

The goal is to find:
- real local businesses
- real digital weakness
- real commercial upside

### Stage 2 — Qualification
Before any demo is built, check:
- are they a good fit?
- are they not a chain / enterprise / ecommerce-heavy?
- is there a visibility gap?
- can SiteForge realistically improve this case?

### Stage 3 — Outreach
Send a concrete business-oriented message.

The message should be about:
- lost calls
- weak conversion
- weak local visibility
- poor website structure

Not about:
- generic design opinions
- aesthetics for aesthetics’ sake

### Stage 4 — Demo generation
Only for strong validated targets.

Once a business is clearly a good fit, SiteForge can generate a demo fast using:
- template + config
- niche-aware structure
- source-site reading
- AI-assisted customization

### Stage 5 — Close and onboard
Once client says yes:
- deploy staging
- onboard domain / assets
- push production
- start maintenance rhythm

### Stage 6 — Maintenance
Ongoing small improvements:
- content refreshes
- hours/phone changes
- blog drafts
- safe staging workflow

## Why maps-first discovery matters to the business

This was a major strategic correction.

If discovery starts from search winners, the business wastes time targeting companies that:
- already rank well
- already have decent digital visibility
- may not need us enough

That creates poor targeting.

Maps-first discovery is better because it helps us find:
- real local operators
- underpowered digital presence
- stronger business upside
- more realistic sales targets

This directly improves:
- lead quality
- conversion rate
- usefulness of demos
- overall business efficiency

So discovery is not a side detail.
It is central to whether the business works at all.

## What makes a good SiteForge customer

A good customer usually has:
- one city / region focus
- clear service offering
- real demand
- weak website or weak conversion structure
- enough reputation that better visibility would matter
- no complex software or ecommerce requirement

Signs of good fit:
- local contractor / field service business
- phone-driven / quote-driven sales
- some reviews already
- weak trust structure online
- no content engine
- no clear local SEO buildout

Signs of bad fit:
- online store is central
- enterprise / chain decision-making
- existing digital team or advanced website stack
- very low upside from replacing current site

## What should NOT happen

The business should not drift into:
- random redesign hobbyism
- building demos for businesses we cannot realistically sell
- targeting companies that are already digitally strong
- trying to replace systems with ecommerce / app complexity
- spending more effort on demo production than on finding real buyers

A demo is only valuable if it supports a real sales path.

---

## The current operational rules

### Rule 1
Do not build demos for poor-fit businesses.

### Rule 2
Do not target chains or enterprise operators.

### Rule 3
Do not let visible search winners dominate discovery.

### Rule 4
Prefer niches where:
- trust matters
- local search matters
- quote requests matter
- content can improve visibility

### Rule 5
Validate discovery quality before scaling demo generation.
